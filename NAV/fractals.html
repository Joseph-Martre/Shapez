Fractals

Fractals are strange mathematical objects that were invented to better describe the innate roughness of natural shapes. Most of them have a non-integer dimension and the most well-known ones are self-similar regardless of scale.

Sierpinski Fractals

The Sierpinski triangle is a very computationally simple fractal. This means it emerges very often in seemingly unrelated fields such as cellular automata and probability.

The Sierpinsky carpet should not be confused with its crypto-currency cousin, the Bankman rugpull.

The lamest fractal of this trio. The Sierpinsky pentagon's edges don't even properly connect with each other making it look very unstable and flakey.

Hall of Fame

The Koch snowflake and its six branches. Not actually as cool as it looks.

Probably the most well-known fractal amongst mathematicians. The Mandelbrot set has a complex shape emerging from a simple definition.

The Julia set is used to characterize the stability of functions applied onto themselves.

Fractals in Nature

Snowflakes owe their shape to the way H2O molecules arrange themselves when water freezes.

Lightning is a naturally occuring 3 dimensional Lichtenberg figure.

Romanesco "broccoli" is actually a variant of cauliflower. This is a real picture and not a 3D render.
