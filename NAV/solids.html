Solid 3D Shapes

We've all been introduced to shapes in the form of painted wooden blocks as kids. If you used to be one of those kids who couldn't fit the shapes in the holes and threw them at their siblings in frustration, you might actually learn something from this page.

Cubes

Also known as a "Regular Hexahedron" if you want to be fancy. A very simple shape that's begging to be inserted into a square hole.

The most well-known net for a square. Children make paper dice from this template.

A 3D projection of a 4D Hypercube known as a Tesseract. Does not actually have any mystical properties.

Spheres

The Sphere is the simplest 3D shape to parametrize with an equation. It is characterized by its radius.

Projections from a plane to a sphere are increasingly warped as the projection rays approach edges.

Celestial bodies tend to approach spherical shapes as they grow larger in order to even out their mass.

Platonic Solids

A few natural crystals have the shape of an octahedron, including fluorite and diamond.

The dodecahedron. The most uninteresting and goofiest-looking platonic solid.

While children often use six-sided die for play, the icosahedron is a 20-sided die used by a far more sinister demographic: tabletop gamers.
